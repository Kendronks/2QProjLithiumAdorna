Helping you setup the client settings for osu!std

Part 1: Ctrl+O

Ctrl + o is the settings command of osu!, it allows you to configure almost everything you need, from audio to video settings, version details, and personalization settings.

Part 2: Navigating the settings

When you press ctrl + o in osu!, a bunch of settings will pop up, and a navbar on the left, segregating all the settings by bulk. Ultimately, you should take a look at each setting and determine your preferred setup, but if you want relatively optimized settings for gameplay, here is a list of my settings shortcuts:

    Renderer settings (Graphics >> Renderer)


  • Frame limiter: Unlimited
  • Show Fps counter: On
  • Compatibility mode: Off
  • Reduce Dropped Frames: Off
  • Detect Perfomance Issues: On

  • General settings (Gameplay >> General)

  • Background Dim: 100%
  • Don't change dim level during breaks: On
  • Show leaderboard during gameplay: Off
  • Progress display: Top-Right (Pie)
  • Score meter type: Hit-error
  • Score meter size: 1x
  • Always show key overlay: Off
  • Show approach circle on first "Hidden" object: On
  • Scale osu!mania scroll speed with bpm: Off
  • Rememember osu!mania scroll speed per beatmap: Off

  • Skin settings (Skin >> Skin)

  • Current skin: - YUGEN FINAL WS (This skin was made by Garin)
  • Ignore all beatmap skins: On
  • Use skin sound samples: Off
  • Use taiko skin for taiko mode: Off
  • Always use skin cursor: On

  • Mouse settings (Skin >> Mouse)

  • Sensitivity: your choice
  • Raw input: For mouse: On. For tablet: Off.
  • Map absolute raw input to the osu! window: Off
  • Confine mouse cursor: only when fullscreen
  • Disable mouse wheel in play mode: On
  • Disable mouse buttons in play mode: On
  • Cursor ripples: Off

Part 3: Windows Optimization

This is optional, but if you want to be more optimized towards gameplay, you can configure your graphics card and windows settings. There are many ways to do this, and I will not be delving into the specifics of this optimization process, but feel free to fiddle around with your graphics card settings. This can be done by pressing right click in the desktop for nvidia and AMD, and selecting the respective graphics card settings. For windows optimization, go to settings >> focus assist, and feel free to turn everything off. You could also disable the Xbox game bar for windows 10 by going to Settings >> Enable Xbox Game Bar, and turning it off.

Final Part: Personalization

This is the part where you configure your settings to match you, selecting your sensitivity, installing beatmaps of your favorite songs on the osu! website, and selecting a skin that suits your taste are some of the most enjoyable parts of the setup experience. Although I must warn you, osu! has a lot of Japanese pop culture references, so if that is not to your liking, feel free to use the defaults and enjoy the game however you wish.
